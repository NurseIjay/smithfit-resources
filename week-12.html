What Changes After 35

5 Shifts That Happen

Metabolism slows (muscle loss accelerates). Recovery takes longer. Stress sensitivity increases (cortisol hits harder). Sleep quality drops. Your body holds water and fat differently, especially the midsection.

"This isn't your body betraying you. It's your body adapting. The rules changed. Your approach needs to change too."

What to Stop Doing

These Worked in Your 20s

Stop: extreme calorie restriction (makes hormonal symptoms worse), excessive cardio (raises cortisol in a system already overloaded), ignoring strength training (muscle is the #1 driver of metabolism after 35), skipping meals (blood sugar instability compounds hormonal shifts).

"Everything that worked in your 20s can actively work against you now."

What to Start Doing

The New Non-Negotiables

Start: strength training 2-3x/week (non-negotiable for metabolism), eating MORE protein (1g per pound of goal body weight), prioritizing sleep over early morning workouts, managing stress as a health intervention not a luxury.

"This isn't about doing more. It's about doing different."

The Hormonal Window

Work With Your Cycle

Your hormones fluctuate weekly and monthly. Some weeks you'll feel unstoppable. Others you'll feel like your body is fighting you. That's not failure. That's biology. Track your energy patterns for one month. Push harder when you feel strong. Back off when you feel depleted.

"Working WITH your cycle instead of against it is the difference between progress and frustration."

Night Shift Note

Night shift amplifies every perimenopausal symptom. Hot flashes are worse when your circadian rhythm is disrupted. Mood swings hit harder on broken sleep. This isn't you being dramatic. It's physiology stacked on physiology. Give yourself more grace on shifts.

Your One Thing This Week

If you can only do ONE thing this week: add one strength training session. Even 15 minutes. Bodyweight squats, push-ups, lunges. Muscle is your metabolism after 35. That's enough. Seriously.
