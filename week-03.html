Week 3 Resource Card

The Chaos Day Survival Kit

When everything falls apart, these 4 rescue plans keep you fueled without thinking.

Some days, the plan goes out the window. Double shifts. Short-staffed floors. Kids, life, chaos. These four rescue plans are your safety net. No prep required. No guilt necessary. Just protein, fuel, and forward.

Drive-Through Rescue

The Smart Drive-Through Order

Grilled chicken sandwich (skip the fries) + side salad or apple slices. Any chain.

~30g protein

You're not failing by hitting a drive-through. You're making the smartest call available to you under pressure. That's not weakness. That's strategy.

Gas Station Rescue

The 60-Second Gas Station Grab

String cheese (2 sticks) + mixed nuts (small bag) + banana + water

~18g protein

Available at literally any gas station between shifts. Not Instagram-worthy. Doesn't need to be.

Break Room Rescue

The Locker Stash

Can of tuna or chicken + crackers + an apple from the vending machine

~25g protein

Keep 3-4 cans in your locker. This is the backup plan that has never let a single nurse down. Boring? Yes. Reliable? Every time.

Frozen Dinner Rescue

The Freezer Aisle Save

Any high-protein frozen meal (aim for 20g+ protein on the label) + bag of frozen veggies microwaved alongside

~24g protein

Read one number on the label: protein. Pick the highest one. Microwave both. Eat. Move on.

Night Shift Angle

The chaos day rescue kit isn't just for bad shifts. It's for that moment at 2am when the cafeteria is closed, the vending machine is your only option, and your body is screaming for sugar. When that happens, your brain is going to tell you to grab the candy bar. That's cortisol talking, not hunger. Grab the nuts and cheese instead. It won't feel as satisfying in the moment, but 30 minutes later, you'll feel steady instead of crashed.

If You Can Only Do One Thing This Week

Put 3 cans of tuna and a bag of nuts in your locker. Right now. Before your next shift. That's your insurance policy.
